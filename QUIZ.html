History

The oldest known evidence of hominid habitation in Jordan dates back at least 200,000 years Jordan is rich in Paleolithic remains (up to 20,000 years old) due to its location within the Leszat where expansions of hominids out of Africa converged.

Geography

Jordan sits strategically at the crossroads of the continents of Asia. Africa and Europe, in the Levant area of the Fertile Crescent, a cradle of civilization.

Climate

The climate in Jordan varies greatly. Generally, the further inland from the editerranean, there are greater contrasts in temperature and less rainfall.

Ecology

Over 2,000 plant species have been recorded in Jordan. Many of the flowering plants bloom in the spring after the winter rains and the type of vegetation depends largely on the levels of precipitation.

Transportation

Jordan is ranked as having the 35th best infrastructure in the world, one of the highest rankings in the developing world, according to the 2010 World Economic Forum's Index of Economic Competitiveness.

This high infrastructural development is necessitated by its role as a transit country for goods and services to Palestine and Irag.
